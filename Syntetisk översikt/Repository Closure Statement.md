## Core Position

The RP9 framework establishes a single, non-iterable axiom and derives from it a necessary geometric and structural consequence chain. Within this framework, **relation is primary**, geometry is structural, and mathematics is a secondary descriptive language.

From this position follows a decisive implication:

> **If RP9 is internally consistent and correct, then the classical Millennium Problems are not correctly formulated as fundamental problems.**

This is not a claim of superiority, dismissal, or rhetorical negation. It is a **logical incompatibility statement**.

## On Logical Incompatibility

The Millennium Problems are posed within a paradigm that implicitly treats mathematical formalism as foundational. RP9 demonstrates that this assumption is not neutral: it is structurally contingent.

During RP9:

- mathematical undecidability is not fundamental, but derivative

- non-existence or singularity pathologies signal incomplete relational closure

- "open problems" arise when formal systems are decoupled from their geometric preconditions


Therefore, the two positions cannot simultaneously hold:

- If the Millennium Problems are fundamentally and correctly posed, then RP9 is false.

- If RP9 is correct, then the Millennium Problems are secondary artifacts of an incomplete formal framing and are invalid as fundamental problem statements.


Both cannot be true at the same time.

## Clarification on the Riemann Z-Structure

One exception requires explicit clarification.

What is commonly referred to as the _Riemann problem_ contains a genuine structural phenomenon — the so-called Z-structure. Under RP9, this is not a paradox or a problem to be solved, but a **geometric function** arising from relational symmetry, spectrum, and residual imbalance.

In this context:

- the Z-structure is not pathological

- it does not demand resolution

- it expresses a lawful geometric manifestation


Treating it as a “problem” is a categorical error.
